# Independent Competitive Strategy Review

## Verdict

Long-term mastery must come from prediction, resource sequencing, environment control, and counter selection. It must not primarily measure drawing speed or external memorization.

## Strategic requirements accepted

- At least five viable strategic identities.
- Soft counters rather than automatic matchups.
- Shared environmental states.
- Three-stage cast telegraphs.
- No silent instant damage.
- Loadout-limited live decision space.
- In-game codex and post-match coaching.
- Public competitive access to all spells before ranked.
- Secret discoveries add mystery without permanent knowledge inequality.
- Anti-stall pressure is symmetric.
- No hidden losing-player damage bonus.

## Matchup principles

- Every archetype needs a favorable, unfavorable, and approximately even matchup.
- Counter advantage should change probability, not remove agency.
- A player must be able to trade, evade, preempt, or cleanse each debuff/control plan.
- Utility spells must become poor choices under some board states.
- Environment specialists are slow to establish but both players may use the resulting state.

## Information rules

- The opponent sees charge count, active environment, major statuses, and resonance.
- Exact cooldown numbers may remain hidden, but cast school and threat class may not.
- Gesture silhouettes must remain legible before release.
- Visual effects yield to enemy heavy-cast tells.
- Sound has an equivalent visual cue.

## Fair comeback policy

No rubber-band damage or defense bonus is recommended. Comebacks emerge from:

- interrupting Focus,
- earning half-charge through a skilled Reflect or interrupt,
- exploiting an opponent's committed resonance,
- turning a shared environment against its creator,
- Arcane Pressure making a passive lead harder to preserve.

## Ranked validation

- Track matchup matrix by skill bracket.
- Sample expert replays to discover loops bots miss.
- Avoid balance changes from small samples.
- Separate low-skill readability problems from high-skill efficiency problems.
- Re-run the complete deterministic simulation set after every balance-data change.
