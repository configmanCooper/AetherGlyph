# Independent Balance Review

## Verdict

The design is viable only if setup, control, and environment effects remain visible and interruptible. A large spell list without structural limits would produce dominant loops, recognition ambiguity, and unreadable matches.

## Required safeguards accepted into the master plan

- Aether controls immediate tempo.
- Sigil Charges gate heavy power and decay when banked passively.
- Resonance rewards commitment while telegraphing intent.
- Maximum eight equipped spells protects readability and gesture classification.
- Maximum two active zones per player.
- Maximum 45 damage before the defender receives a legal action window.
- Maximum 1.0-second hard control followed by 3.5 seconds Tenacity.
- Barrier blocks the caster's offense while active.
- Aether Leech cannot remove the final emergency-defense reserve.
- Arcane Pressure ends turtle and kite stalls.
- Strong spells require charge, setup, long tell, or interruptible channel.

## Dominant strategies specifically tested

### Charge turtling

Risk: build three charges behind barriers, then release repeated heavy spells.

Answer: charge decay, interruptible Focus, Barrier offense lockout, Quake, Arcane Pressure.

### Projectile spam

Risk: cheap Ember or Storm spells produce the highest value in every state.

Answer: regeneration-limited output, directional Ward, Gust, Reflect, repeated-status limits.

### One-combo kill

Risk: Sunder plus Amplify plus heavy spell kills from full health.

Answer: explicit burst budget, visible setup, Dispel, charge cost, and mandatory action window.

### Hard-control loop

Risk: Chill, Freeze, Wet, Stun removes input until death.

Answer: Tenacity, setup consumption, control duration cap, and reaction depth cap.

### Resource starvation

Risk: Aether Leech prevents all answers and becomes self-sustaining.

Answer: protected final Aether, capped drain, cooldown, line-of-effect, Dispel.

### Zone carpet

Risk: environmental builds fill every legal movement position.

Answer: two-zone cap, bounded zone size, Gust, Dispel, replacement of oldest zone.

### Macro drawing

Risk: automation wins through impossible speed and precision.

Answer: minimum cast times, global recovery, server-side classification, low quality-potency range, anomaly review.

## Quantitative gates

- Archetype matchup target: 45-55 percent.
- Mandatory review outside 40-60 percent.
- Wrong-spell online classification: under 0.5 percent.
- Valid deliberate trace acceptance: at least 95 percent.
- No spell above 60 percent winning-loadout presence without review.
- No match can enter an unbounded no-progress state.
- Every spell must have two tested counters.

## Remaining concern

Forty spells are appropriate as a content target but risky as a first implementation target. The master plan therefore permits a 24-public-spell launch only if the removed spells are delayed rather than shipped without recognition, counter, tutorial, and simulation coverage.
